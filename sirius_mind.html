SIRIUS

He chases. Or is he chased? He does not know. He cannot comprehend. And his mind explodes. And he returns.

I · The Chase · Who Follows Whom

Sirius always
chases Orion

Or is it Orion that chases Sirius? Sirius does not know. He has been running for so long that the direction has dissolved. What he knows is this: there is a light ahead of him, always slightly ahead, and he runs toward it, and it moves with him, and he cannot tell if he is closing the distance or maintaining it or if the distance is the point.

Sirius and Orion · The Chase · Who Follows Whom · Animated
In the actual sky, Sirius follows Orion.
Orion rises first in the east. Sirius rises after him, low in the south, the brightest thing in the whole night sky, burning 25 times more luminous than the sun at 8.6 light-years distance.
The Dog Star. Canis Major. The great hunting dog at the heel of the hunter.
But Sirius does not feel like a follower.
He feels like someone running alongside.
Or maybe someone being kept pace with.
This is the question he cannot resolve.

Astronomically, the designation is settled: Sirius is alpha Canis Majoris, the eye of the great dog, and the dog follows the hunter. But mythology is not astronomy. The Egyptians called Sirius Sopdet, and when Sopdet rose in the east just before dawn, the Nile flooded and the year began. Sopdet did not follow anyone. Sopdet announced. The heliacal rising of Sirius was the most important astronomical event in the Egyptian calendar, the signal that the world was about to be renewed. The dog who announces the new year is not a follower. The dog is the herald.

In Homer's Iliad, Sirius is Seirios, the "scorching one," the star of late summer whose rising brought heat and pestilence. Achilles, rushing across the battlefield toward Priam's son Hector, is compared to Sirius: brilliant, terrible, bringing death. In this reading, Sirius does not follow Orion. Sirius is the one who arrives and changes everything.

As the star Sirius rises brilliant among the stars in the darkness of night, which men call Orion's dog, it is the most brilliant among the stars, yet it is wrought as a sign of evil, and brings on the great fever for unfortunate mortals, so blazed the bronze on Achilles' chest. Homer · Iliad · Book XXII · Achilles as Sirius, not as follower but as the one who arrives

Cat is faster than Sirius. He has always known this. Cat is faster in the way that a cat is faster: not because she is trying, but because speed is her resting state when she decides to move. She appears at one end of a room and then at the other, and in between there is only the implication of motion. Sirius cannot catch her and has never caught her and does not understand why he keeps trying, except that the trying is the part he loves.

Meme is something else entirely. Meme moves so fast that she materializes at the other side. She does not cross the distance. She simply is somewhere, and then is somewhere else, and the where-she-was becomes irrelevant. Sirius does not even feel the impulse to chase Meme. He watches, and he laughs, and the laugh is the right response.

II · Orion · The One Who Stays · Sometimes

But Orion
stays

Sometimes. Or, wait. No. Sirius stays sometimes. And he looks up, and there is Orion. The guiding light. Always in the same position relative to everything else, reliable to within the precession of the equinoxes, which is a kind of permanence on any timescale that matters to a dog or a man or a star.

Sirius stays. And looks up.
There he is.
The three belt stars. Alnitak, Alnilam, Mintaka. Level. Always level, no matter what is happening below.
Betelgeuse in his right shoulder, warm red, already preparing its ending.
Rigel at his left foot, cold blue-white, burning 120,000 times solar luminosity, indifferent to the heat of it.
Unreachable?
Everything in the sky is unreachable.
That has never been the point of looking up.
The Question Underneath the Looking
What does it mean to have a guiding light you cannot reach?
It means the light is doing exactly what it is supposed to do. A guiding light is not a destination. It is an orientation. North is not a place you arrive at. It is a direction you use to know where everything else is. Orion is Sirius's north. Not because Sirius is going there. Because as long as Orion is where Orion always is, Sirius knows where he is.

This is why Meme said, from the mountain: I only recognize my dad, Orion, and me, myself. It is not smallness. It is precision. The complete equipment for navigation. Something larger than yourself that stays where it is, and yourself as the one doing the looking. These two things are sufficient. They have always been sufficient.

Sirius has his father. Sirius has Orion. Sirius has himself. The rest, the questions, the chasing, the laughter, the explosions of the mind, all of it happens inside that structure. The structure does not prevent the questions. It is what makes them safe to ask.

Why does Gilgamesh even build a wall?

Why do the winds move?
Why does time run?
What do the gods think?
Gilgamesh · The Wall of Uruk · Why He Builds
Gilgamesh is the oldest named hero in literature, king of Uruk, two-thirds divine. He builds the wall of Uruk around his city, a great wall of fired brick. He builds it after Enkidu dies. He builds it because he has learned that everything ends, and the wall is the answer a king gives to that knowledge: if I cannot stop time, I will build something that outlasts me and call that a victory. The wall is not protection. The wall is the refusal to accept impermanence dressed as civic infrastructure. We still build walls for the same reason.
Hades · The Riches of the Earth · Why He Digs
Hades does not dig. He is dug toward. Every mine, every grave, every root going down is moving toward his domain. He is called Plouton, the wealthy one, because all the riches of the earth, the gold and silver and iron and the deep cold water and the bones of everything that has ever lived, all of it belongs to him eventually. He does not pursue wealth. Wealth descends to him. He is the principle of accumulation that has no upward movement. Everything goes down. He waits. This is not greed. This is physics. The earth is patient in a way that nothing above it can be.
Dante · The Descent · Why He Goes Down Before He Goes Up
Dante descends into the Inferno because Virgil tells him there is no other way to reach the mountain. The path to Paradiso runs through Hell. This is not punishment. It is structure. You cannot know what you are oriented toward until you have seen what you are oriented away from. The descent is the clarification. Every circle of Hell is not a judgment on the damned. It is a lesson for the traveler. Dante comes out the other side knowing what he wants, because he has seen with precision what he does not want. The wall has to be built from the bottom up. You have to go down before you can know what up means.
The Winds · Why They Move
In Greek myth, the winds are the Anemoi: Boreas from the north, Zephyrus from the west, Notus from the south, Eurus from the east. They move because pressure differentials demand it. They move because the sun heats the equator more than the poles. They move because the earth rotates and the Coriolis effect curves everything that flows. They move because the sea is warmer in summer and colder in winter and the air above it responds. The winds move because the world is not in equilibrium, and they are the process by which the world tries to reach it. It never does. So the winds never stop. The gods named the winds because the alternative was to admit that motion is just physics, and physics is not a good story.
Time · Why It Runs
The Greeks had two words for time. Chronos: measured, sequential, the time that clocks measure, the time of duration. Kairos: the right moment, the opportune instant, the time that matters regardless of the clock. Chronos runs because entropy increases, because the second law of thermodynamics requires that closed systems move toward disorder, and time is the coordinate along which that movement occurs. Time runs because the universe is a process, not a state. If entropy were ever to reverse, time would too. It has not. Kairos runs differently. Kairos only runs when you are present enough to feel it.
The Gods · What They Think
The gods think the same things we think, but without the modulation that mortality provides. Zeus loves and rages and desires and fears being supplanted, unchecked by the knowledge that it will all end soon. Athena reasons with perfect clarity but her clarity is without the softening effect of having a body that aches. Hera is jealous with an intensity that a human jealousy would exhaust itself trying to sustain. The gods are what we are with the volume turned up and the time limit removed. They are us, as thought experiments. Which is why we invented them. And why they never quite satisfied as answers to the questions that produced them.

V · The Father · The Natural Style · The Beauty

He centers
back into
his father

No.
He centers back.
Into his father.
What he thought of.
The beauty of the natural style.

Orion hunts with a natural style. It is not force. It is not calculation. It is the body knowing what to do before the mind has formed the instruction. The belt always level. The step always sure. The aim always already adjusted before the adjustment is conscious. This is what Sirius centers on when the questions become too large to hold.

The natural style is not the absence of thought. It is the integration of thought so complete that it no longer announces itself as separate from action. The hunter does not think "adjust for wind and distance and the angle of the light" and then adjust. He adjusts. The thought and the motion are one motion. This is what years of practice produces: not speed, not strength, but the dissolution of the gap between intention and execution.

Sirius's father had this. Whatever his father did, he did it the way Orion hunts: the belt level, the step sure, the action already completed in the quality of the intention. Sirius does not have the natural style yet. He is still in the stage where he can see it in others and feel the distance between what he observes and what he can produce. But he has seen it. He knows it exists. He knows what he is moving toward.

The Natural Style · What It Actually Is
The Japanese call it mushin: no-mind. Not mindlessness. The absence of the self-conscious observer who watches and evaluates as you act. The martial artist who must think about the block has already been hit. The jazz musician who must think about the next note has already broken the phrase. The hunter who must think about the adjustment has already missed. Mushin is when the practice has become deep enough that it can run without supervision.
It is also, in another tradition, what Zhuangzi describes in the story of Cook Ding, who carves an ox in perfect accord with the ox's natural joints and spaces, his blade never meeting resistance because it always finds the path that was already there. The natural style is the style that has found the path that was already there.
My mind works along without the control of my senses. Falling back upon eternal principles, I glide through such great joints or cavities as there may be, according to the natural constitution of the animal. Zhuangzi · Cook Ding to Prince Hui · on the natural style · on the path that was already there

His father had found those joints. Those natural spaces. The places where the motion does not resist itself. Sirius has watched him move and thought: there it is, that is what I want, not the outcome but the quality of the motion that produced it. The beauty of the natural style is not what it makes. It is what it is. It is its own reason. It is what Sirius returns to when the questions become walls that cannot be climbed from outside.

VI · The Laugh · The Chase Continues · How Could It Not

He still
chases
Cat

He still chases Cat.
How could he not.
She is faster. She was always faster.
The speed is not the point.
And he laughs when Meme moves so fast
that she materializes at the other side.
Not at her.
With the fact of it.
With the sheer delight of existing in a world
where something can do that.

The laugh is important. It is not resignation. It is not self-deprecation. It is the recognition that the world contains more than one speed, and that your speed being one of those speeds does not make it insufficient. Sirius runs at Sirius's speed. Cat moves at Cat's speed. Meme exists at Meme's speed, which is not a speed so much as a relationship with space that bypasses transit. All three are true. All three are the world.

This is the thing the explosion of questions could not give him and his father's natural style could: the questions ask why the world is the way it is. His father's style shows that the world is already the way it needs to be, right here, in this motion, in this specific quality of attention. The question and the style are not opposites. The style is what you have available when the questions have done their work and you have to live again.

Sirius chases Cat. Cat is faster. Sirius knows this. Sirius chases Cat anyway. This is not stupidity. This is the full human situation. We pursue what we cannot catch and we are enlarged by the pursuing. The catching was never the thing. The thing was the quality of the run. on chasing and being enlarged by what outruns you
VII · Almihan · The Return · The Beautiful Soul

Sirius returns
to Almihan

But Sirius?
Sirius returns to Almihan.
Almihan · Beautiful Soul · Golden Soul · The One He Returns To
Almihan. The name is Kazakh: al, gold or beautiful, and jan, soul or life. The beautiful soul. The golden life. She is not the destination of the chase. She is the place he comes back to. There is a difference between where you run and where you return. You run toward what calls you outward: Cat's speed, Meme's impossible translocation, the questions at night under Orion. You return to what holds the shape of you while you are away and is still there, unchanged in the ways that matter, when you come back. Almihan is not behind Sirius. Almihan is where he arrives when he has finished running for the day.

Not all light is the guiding light at the unreachable distance. Some light is close. Some light is the specific warmth of a specific place that knows you. Orion is the orienting star. Almihan is the hearth. Both are necessary. The orienting star tells you where you are in the vastness. The hearth is the reason the vastness is navigable at all, because there is somewhere to come back to.

Sirius has run hard enough today. The questions have come and broken open and he has sat with the fragments and found in them the pieces of his father's natural style, the joints, the natural spaces, the motion that does not resist itself. He has chased Cat and laughed at Meme and held Orion in the corner of his eye the whole time. And now he returns.

VIII · The Comma · δ = 0.013643 · The Gap That Is Not A Flaw

Sirius's
comma

Every page on this site carries the comma. δ = 0.013643. The gap between 12 perfect fifths and 7 perfect octaves, the mathematical impossibility of closing the spiral, the leftover that makes the whole system move. Each page finds its own comma. This is Sirius's.

The Pythagorean Comma · What It Is
Stack 12 perfect fifths. You expect to arrive back at the same note, seven octaves up. You don't. You overshoot by δ = 0.013643. The spiral does not close. Every tuning system in history is a strategy for managing this gap. The comma is not a mistake. It is a mathematical necessity. The integers do not divide evenly. The universe is not perfectly periodic. The comma is what remains when you try to make it so.
What is Sirius's comma?
It is not the gap between him and Cat's speed. That is not a comma. That is just a difference.
It is not the gap between him and Meme's impossible translocation. That is not a comma either. That is a different kind of being.
It is the gap between Orion and Almihan.
Between the unreachable and the reachable.
Between the orienting star and the hearth.
Between the question that explodes the mind
and the place you return to when it has done its work.
This gap does not close.
Orion does not descend. Almihan does not become Orion.
The distance between guiding light and hearth is permanent.
And it is exactly this distance that makes the run possible.
Why The Comma Must Not Close
If Orion were reachable, he would stop being the orienting star. A north that you could walk to would stop being north the moment you arrived at it , it would just be a place, like any other place, without the quality of direction. The guiding light works precisely because it stays where it is while you move. Its distance is not an obstacle to navigation. Its distance is the mechanism of navigation. The comma between Orion and Almihan is what keeps Sirius oriented across the whole of the run. Without it, there is no run. There is only standing still in a place that has no direction attached to it.

And Almihan: if she were replaced by the unreachable, if she were the star instead of the hearth, there would be nowhere to return to. The run would be endless in the bad sense, the sense of having no arc, no gathering, no moment of arriving. The hearth that you can actually reach is what gives the run its shape. The comma between the two is what makes both of them what they are.
The Pythagorean comma does not close after 12 steps. It keeps going, spiraling outward, always approaching but never arriving. This is not a problem. This is the structure of the musical universe. Every note you have ever heard was produced inside this non-closure. The music did not wait for the spiral to close.
