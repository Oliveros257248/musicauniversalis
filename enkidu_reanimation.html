The Design Language

Every aesthetic decision was made deliberately. Here is the grammar.

Color Palette — CSS Variables
--gold: #c9a84c / #e8c97a → Mathematics · Philosophy · Music
--teal: #2dd4bf → Instruments · SETI · Protocol
--green: #4caf7d → Biology · Ecology · Rescue Plan
--purple: #a07ecf → Archive · Challenges · Enkidu
--blue: #5b9bd5 → Physics · Space · Gravity
--pink: #e06080 → Chemistry · DFT · Molecules
--red: #d94f4f → Climate emergency · Doomsday

Typography: Cinzel Decorative (section titles and headings — the ancient, the formal), EB Garamond (body text, italic for poetry and quotes), Space Mono (data, file names, labels, everything that is measured). Never Inter or Arial. Never system fonts on the pages that carry the theory.

Body font size must be at least 16px — ideally 18px. Labels and monospace tags can go to 0.75rem but must be legible. The work is meant to be read by everyone from a curious child to a physicist. No text should require squinting.

Backgrounds: Deep navy (#07090e or #08090f). Starfield canvas animations — 180–200 stars, pulsing alpha, hsla color variations. Radial gradients at section breaks. Never pure black. Never white.

Sections: Subject color accent on left border (3px solid). Card grids: 3-column default, responsive to 1-column mobile. Hover: translateY(-2px) and slight background lightening. Never scale transforms that shift layout.

Quotes: Large. Italic. EB Garamond. Font size 1.2rem–1.4rem. Left border 4px. Light tinted background. Attribution in Space Mono small caps. The giant decorative opening quote mark in pseudo-element. Quotes are architectural, not decorative — they carry the philosophical weight.
