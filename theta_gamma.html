Section I · The Two Rhythms

⚐ COMMA FRAMEWORK ARTICULATION · neural oscillation ratio as comma
A brain that plays
in two keys at once

Place yourself inside a hippocampus. Two rhythms dominate everything. The first is theta, a slow, sweeping oscillation at 6–10 Hz, generated by the medial septum and synchronized across the entire hippocampal-entorhinal circuit. It is the metronome. It paces the architecture of time. The second is gamma, a fast, sharp oscillation at 30–100 Hz, emerging locally from networks of pyramidal cells and inhibitory interneurons. It is the melody line. It marks the instant of activation, the moment a cell assembly fires together and codes for a thing.

The relationship between them is the subject of this page. Not the rhythms in isolation, both have been studied for decades, but the ratio between them, and what that ratio has to do with the Pythagorean comma.

θ · 4–10 Hz
Theta Oscillation
The slow carrier wave. One theta cycle = 100–250 ms. Synchronized across hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, prefrontal cortex during active navigation and memory encoding.
γ · 30–100 Hz
Gamma Oscillation
The fast carrier. Multiple gamma cycles nest within a single theta cycle. Each gamma subcycle = one active cell assembly = one memory item in working memory.
γ/θ · ~5–10
The Ratio, Not an Integer
The number of gamma cycles per theta cycle. Typically 5–10, often near 7±2 (Miller's working memory capacity). The key: this ratio is never precisely integer.
The Comma of Neural Time
If gamma and theta ran at an exact integer ratio, say, exactly 5 gamma cycles per theta cycle, the two rhythms would lock in perfect synchrony, fire at the same phase on every theta cycle, and create a static, crystalline code with no capacity for novelty or update. The brain does not do this. The measured ratio fluctuates around a non-integer value, typically near 5.1–6.8 depending on region, task, and state. The residue, the non-closure between the slow and fast rhythms, is what allows the system to update its memory contents from cycle to cycle. The theta-gamma code is a managed non-closure. A neural Pythagorean comma.

Section III · Phase Precession

The moving window
of place cells

O'Keefe and Recce discovered in 1993 that as a rat moves through a place cell's field, that cell fires at progressively earlier phases of the ongoing theta cycle. The cell enters the field firing near the theta trough, and exits near the peak, a full precession of roughly 360° across 5–10 theta cycles. This phase precession encodes the rat's spatial trajectory as a temporal code: the sequence of positions the animal will pass through is compressed into the sequence of gamma subcycles within a single theta cycle.

The key mathematical condition for phase precession to work is that the intracellular oscillation driving the cell must run at a slightly higher frequency than theta, not twice as fast, not half as fast, but faster by a small, non-integer residue. The cell's preferred firing phase drifts forward through theta because of this small mismatch. The mismatch is the mechanism. The comma is the precession.

▬ Theta envelope ▬ Cell firing phase · precession path

This is the Pythagorean comma expressed in neural time. The slow theta rhythm completes a cycle. The fast intracellular oscillation has completed slightly more than an integer number of cycles. The residue, the gap, is not averaged away. It accumulates, creating a continuous precession that encodes position with a precision of ~3 cm in a rat's environment, far beyond what a simple rate code could achieve.

Section IV · The θ-γ Code

Working memory
as subcycles

Lisman and Idiart proposed in 1995 that working memory capacity is set by the number of gamma cycles within a theta cycle. Each gamma subcycle contains one active cell assembly, one memory item. The famous limit of 7 ± 2 items (Miller, 1956) arises because a theta cycle at ~8 Hz contains 5–10 gamma subcycles at 40–80 Hz. The ratio γ/θ is not arbitrary. It is the architecture of working memory.

Section V · Pathology

When the ratio
locks or collapses

If the theta-gamma non-integer ratio is the mechanism of sequence encoding, then diseases of that ratio should manifest as diseases of memory and sequence. The evidence points precisely here.

Alzheimer's Disease
Gamma power in CA1 collapses as amyloid accumulates. 40 Hz gamma entrainment (Iaccarino et al., Nature 2016) reduced amyloid and improved cognition in mice, direct evidence that restoring the fast oscillation is therapeutic. The theta-gamma code cannot operate without the fast carrier. Loss of gamma = loss of memory slot resolution.
Schizophrenia
The most replicated finding in schizophrenia electrophysiology: reduced 40 Hz gamma power and disrupted theta-gamma coupling. The predictive coding model of schizophrenia (Friston, 2005) describes the disease as a collapse of the prediction error signal, the gap between expected and received, which is exactly the gap that the theta-gamma non-integer ratio maintains.
Parkinson's Disease
The pathological oscillation in Parkinson's is exaggerated beta synchrony (13–35 Hz) in the basal ganglia-cortical circuit, not theta-gamma disruption per se. But the mechanism is identical: the beta band locks in synchrony rather than remaining in productive non-closure. Deep brain stimulation works by disrupting the locked oscillation, reinstating the gap.
Autism Spectrum
Theta-gamma coupling differences in ASD reflect altered E/I (excitatory/inhibitory) ratio, which directly sets the gamma frequency. A different gamma frequency means a different θ/γ ratio, not necessarily a broken ratio, but a differently tuned one that may not match the external social-communication environment. A different comma placement, not an absent comma.

Section VI · The Resonance Number

Nres = 73.3
in neural time

The resonance number N_res = 1/δ = 73.3 emerges from the Pythagorean comma: after 73.3 iterations of the 3:2 ratio, the accumulated non-closure equals exactly one full octave gap. In the theta-gamma system, the analogous question is: after how many theta cycles does the phase-precessing cell assembly return to its starting phase?

For a γ/θ ratio of R, the answer is 1/(R − floor(R)) theta cycles. For R = 40/8 = 5.0, the answer is ∞, perfect lock, no return, static code. For R = 40.8/8 = 5.1, the answer is 10 theta cycles, the system resets every ~1.25 seconds. For R = 73.3/8 = 9.16..., the phase reset period equals N_res in theta units, and the non-closure per cycle is exactly δ. The Pythagorean comma is the natural non-closure of the neural oscillator optimized for KAM stability near 3:2 coupling.

Specifically: hippocampal recordings during optimal memory performance should show γ/θ ratios with fractional parts near δ = 0.013643 or its multiples. Phase precession measured across many place fields should show mean precession rates consistent with this residue. This is a quantitative prediction, not a metaphor.

Section VIII · What Remains Unresolved

Honest obstacles

The precise non-integer ratio is not yet pinned. The θ/γ ratio varies by region, task, state, and species. The claim that it converges to a value with fractional part near δ = 0.013643 is a prediction, not a confirmed finding. No paper has yet measured this with the precision required to distinguish δ from nearby values.
Phase-phase coupling is contested. Scheffer-Teixeira and Tort (eLife 2016) demonstrated that previously reported n:m phase-phase coupling between theta and gamma may be artifactual, produced by filtering, waveform asymmetry, and improper surrogate methods. Phase-amplitude coupling is well-established; phase-phase coupling is not.
The N_res connection is structural, not yet empirical. The argument that N_res = 73.3 is the natural phase-reset period of the theta-gamma system is mathematically coherent but has not been directly tested in hippocampal recordings. It is a prediction arising from the CPCS framework, not a replicated finding.
The 7±2 argument requires scrutiny. Miller's 1956 working memory capacity limit is a rough empirical observation, and the precise mapping to theta-gamma subcycles depends on both theta frequency and gamma frequency varying together. The relationship is suggestive, not proven.
